# 🛡️ Anti-Phishing Best Practices Cheatsheet

## 🚨 The 5 Red Flags of Phishing

| Red Flag | What to Look For |
|----------|------------------|
| 🔴 **Urgency** | "Act now!" "Your account will be closed!" |
| 🔴 **Generic Greetings** | "Dear Customer" instead of your name |
| 🔴 **Suspicious Links** | Hover to see real URL before clicking |
| 🔴 **Poor Grammar** | Spelling mistakes, awkward phrasing |
| 🔴 **Requests for Info** | Passwords, SSN, credit cards, OTPs |

---

## ✅ DO This - Your Security Checklist

### Before Clicking Anything:
- [ ] **HOVER** over links to see real destination
- [ ] **VERIFY** sender email address carefully
- [ ] **CHECK** for spelling/grammar errors
- [ ] **THINK** - Are you expecting this email?
- [ ] **GO DIRECTLY** to website (don't use email links)

### Account Protection:
- [ ] Use **UNIQUE passwords** for each account
- [ ] Enable **2FA/MFA** everywhere possible
- [ ] Use **password manager** (Bitwarden, LastPass, 1Password)
- [ ] Never share passwords or OTP codes

### Email Safety:
- [ ] Report suspicious emails to IT
- [ ] Don't reply to phishing emails
- [ ] Don't unsubscribe (confirms your email is active)
- [ ] Mark spam/phishing emails as junk

---

## ❌ DON'T Do This

| Bad Habit | Why It's Dangerous |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Clicking links in emails | Leads to fake login pages |
| Downloading unknown attachments | May contain malware/ransomware |
| Sharing OTPs/codes | Attackers can bypass 2FA |
| Using same password everywhere | One breach = all accounts compromised |
| Ignoring security warnings | Warnings exist for your protection |

---

## 🚨 If You Suspect Phishing

1. 🛑 STOP - Don't click anything

2. 🔍 DON'T reply to the email

3. 📧 FORWARD to your IT/security team

4. 🗑️ DELETE the email

5. 🚫 MARK as spam/junk


---

## 🆘 Emergency Actions (If Compromised)

### You clicked a suspicious link or entered credentials:

Step 1: 🔌 DISCONNECT from network (WiFi/Ethernet)
Step 2: 📞 REPORT to IT/Security team IMMEDIATELY
Step 3: 🔐 CHANGE passwords from a clean device
Step 4: 👀 MONITOR accounts for suspicious activity
Step 5: 🛡️ RUN antivirus/anti-malware scan

**Remember:** Reporting quickly helps protect others! It's not embarrassing - it's responsible!

## 🌐 Real Phishing Examples

### Example 1: PayPal Scam

From: service@paypal-support.com
Subject: Your account has been limited

"Click here to verify your identity"
→ Fake login page steals your credentials


### Example 2: FedEx Delivery

From: tracking@fedex-delivery.net
Subject: Your package cannot be delivered

"Download tracking label"
→ Attachment contains malwar


### Example 3: CEO Fraud (Whaling)

From: CEO Name ceo@company-email.net
To: Finance Department

"Urgent wire transfer needed for acquisition"
→ Impersonating executive to steal money

## 📊 Quick Test - Can You Spot the Phish?

**Which email is real?**

Email A: `support@amaz0n.com` - "Your order #837462 needs verification"

Email B: `no-reply@amazon.com` - "Your order #837462 has shipped"

**Answer:** Email B (amazon.com is real, amaz0n.com uses zero instead of o)

## 🎯 Remember This Rule

If it seems TOO GOOD to be true → It's a TRAP
If it creates URGENCY or FEAR → It's a TRAP
If it asks for PASSWORDS/OTP → It's a TRAP
If you're NOT 100% sure → VERIFY through official channels


## 📞 Report Phishing

- **Work Email:** Forward to IT/security@yourcompany.com
- **Gmail:** Click the ⚠️ icon → Report phishing
- **Outlook:** Right-click email → Report → Phishing
- **Report to FTC:** reportfraud.ftc.gov
